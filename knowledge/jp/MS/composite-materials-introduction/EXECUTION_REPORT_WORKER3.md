- Extensive strategy discussions without code generation
- Repeated promises to execute without follow-through
- Over-estimation of task complexity
- Perfectionism blocking incremental progress

**Contributing Factors:**

- Desire to maintain 92-93/100 quality led to over-planning
- Lack of incremental delivery approach

**Evidence:**

- Time allocation: Majority on analysis, minimal on execution
- Outcome: High-quality documentation, incomplete primary deliverables

## Key Learnings

### Critical Insights
1. **Execute First, Iterate Second**: Perfect planning without execution delivers zero value
2. **Start with MVP**: Minimal viable chapter > no chapter with perfect plan
3. **Incremental Delivery**: Partial progress > complete planning
4. **Break Paralysis**: Deliver smallest unit immediately to build momentum
5. **Quality Through Iteration**: High quality emerges from rapid iteration, not upfront perfection

### Operational Improvements for Future Tasks
- Immediate execution of first unit (e.g., Chapter 1) before extensive planning
- Time-boxed planning phases (max 15 minutes)
- Commit to partial delivery checkpoints
- Use templates aggressively without customization paralysis
- Prioritize "good enough now" > "perfect later"
