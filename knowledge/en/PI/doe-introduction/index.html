FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)

Q1: What's the difference between DOE and machine learning optimization?

A: DOE efficiently evaluates factor effects with few experiments and finds statistically valid optimal conditions. Machine learning learns complex patterns from large amounts of data, but DOE is effective when experiment count is limited. Both can be combined.

Q2: Can I understand without statistics knowledge?

A: Basic statistical understanding (mean, variance, hypothesis testing concepts) is desirable. This series explains necessary statistical concepts, but foundational statistics knowledge helps with F-test and p-value interpretation.

Q3: How to choose between orthogonal arrays and RSM?

A: Orthogonal arrays are suitable for factor screening (identifying important factors), while RSM is suitable for optimization after important factors are identified. Typically, narrow down factors with orthogonal arrays, then perform detailed optimization with RSM.

Q4: When should Taguchi methods be used?

A: Effective when wanting to minimize product or process variation. For example, Taguchi method robust design is suitable when maintaining stable quality despite material lot or environmental condition variations.

Q5: What are considerations when applying DOE in actual plants?

A: Important to consider safety, cost, and operational impact. Recommended to reduce experiment count with fractional designs or conduct preliminary evaluations with simulations. Coordination with field operators is also essential.

Q6: What should I learn next after this series?

A: We recommend the following topics:
- Bayesian Optimization: Efficient optimization with few experiments
- Mixture Experimental Design: Optimizing formulation ratios
- Model Predictive Control (MPC): Integration of optimization and control
- Fusion with Machine Learning: Surrogate models and active learning
