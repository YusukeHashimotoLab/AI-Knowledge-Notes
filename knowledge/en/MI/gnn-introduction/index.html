Series Overview

This series is a comprehensive 5-chapter educational content that systematically teaches practical skills for applying Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) to materials science and chemistry, from foundational concepts to advanced applications.

Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) are an innovative technology that represents molecules and crystals as graph structures where "atoms = vertices" and "bonds = edges," enabling deep learning to predict material properties. GNNs automatically learn complex interactions between atoms and spatial configurations that traditional material descriptors (composition, density, symmetry, etc.) cannot capture.
