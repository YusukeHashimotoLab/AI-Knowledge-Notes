ğŸ¯ Series Overview

Quantum Field Theory (QFT) is a theoretical framework that forms the foundation of particle physics and many-body physics. This series covers from the basics of field quantization by canonical quantization and path integrals, to free field theory (scalar fields, Dirac fields, electromagnetic fields), interacting field theory, Feynman diagram techniques, and renormalization theory, learning through a combination of theory and numerical simulation (Python/NumPy/SymPy). This is an advanced course with applications to many-body problems in condensed matter physics, solid-state physics, and materials science in mind.

ğŸ“‹ Learning Objectives

  • Understand and implement field quantization by canonical quantization and path integral formalism
  • Derive Green's functions and propagators for free scalar fields, Dirac fields, and electromagnetic fields
  • Calculate scattering amplitudes and S-matrix in interacting fields
  • Systematically perform perturbative calculations using Feynman diagram techniques
  • Understand the basics of renormalization theory and treatment of ultraviolet divergences, and apply them to many-body problems in materials science
