Global Research & Innovation Plans

A quick reference to the latest priorities of the U.S., EU, China, and Japan—summarized by focus areas, funding tools, milestones, and regulation—so you can draft collaborations and funding strategies faster.

Updated: Jan 2025 Review cadence: Semiannual Audience: Researchers / industry liaison / policy partners

Three headlines to track

Read national plans through three lenses: depth of investment, responsible AI rules, and the strength of shared infrastructure (talent and compute).

  • Foundation spend: Long-horizon funding for semiconductors, quantum, and AI compute is expanding everywhere.
  • Governance: Safety, ethics, and transparency are being codified (e.g., EU AI Act) alongside deployment.
  • International links: More joint calls and mobility programs are opening, creating new collaboration slots.

Country snapshots

United States

CHIPS and Science Act / National AI Initiative

  • Focus: Rebuilding semiconductor capacity, NAIRR pilot for AI compute, quantum and advanced communications.
  • Funding: Federal research boosts combined with state and corporate matching funds.
  • Signature: Open science paired with safety evaluation (AI Safety Institute) in procurement and standards.

European Union

Horizon Europe / Digital Europe / EU AI Act

  • Focus: Green transition, health, manufacturing DX, robotics, and trustworthy AI deployment.
  • Funding: Horizon Europe (2021-2027, ~€95B) provides cross-cutting support for AI and quantum.
  • Signature: Regulation-led model with mandatory transparency plus real-world testbeds.

China

Next Generation AI Plan / 14th Five-Year Plan

  • Focus: Generative AI, smart manufacturing, smart cities, and integration with satellite/communication infrastructure.
  • Funding: Central strategic funds combined with provincial programs to accelerate applied pilots.
  • Signature: Centralized data and compute platforms with parallel regulatory guidelines.

Japan

AI Strategy / Integrated Innovation Strategy / STI Basic Plan

  • Focus: Safe use of generative AI, semiconductors (LSTC/Rapidus), quantum and photonic computing, measurement DX.
  • Funding: Moonshot R&D, GX innovation programs, and industry-academia co-creation platforms.
  • Signature: Safety guidelines plus expanded joint calls with the U.S., EU, and Southeast Asia.

How to use this for collaborations

Align proposals with each country's focus, guardrails, and funding schemes to strengthen competitiveness and create partnership options.
  • Funding strategyTrack domestic calls plus Horizon Europe and U.S. federal joint opportunities.
  • Safety by designEmbed AI safety and data governance early to ease review in international projects.
  • Compute accessBlend shared resources (NAIRR pilot, EuroHPC, Japanese cloud/HPC) for reproducible workflows.
  • MobilityUse short fellowships or double-degree tracks to grow networks in AI, quantum, and semiconductors.
